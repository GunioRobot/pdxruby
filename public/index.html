The Portland Ruby Brigade

Who are we?

pdx.rb is a gathering of Ruby programmers in the Portland, Oregon, area.

Join us!

We meet the first Tuesday (think Ruby Tuesday!) of every month from 7–10pm at FreeGeek (1731 SE 10th Avenue, Portland, OR [map]).

We have an online calendar you can subscribe to (here’s an alternative http: link if you need it). Email events@pdxruby.org with additions.

You can join the mailing list.

You can also find some of us on our IRC channel at #pdx.rb on irc.freenode.net.

Recent events

In August, we put on FOSCON (‘Free OSCON’), a guerilla gathering of Ruby folks from O’Reilly’s OSCON 2005 conference. There are some great photos on Flickr, and some blog coverage by Scott Laird and _why.

Upcoming events

On Saturday, September 17, we will be holding a codefest to build the next version of this website. We want to have:

  • an event planner (‘cat herder’) application that will help us coordinate meetings and other events
  • member journals/blogs, with an aggregator, so you can subscribe to the pdx.rb RSS feed
  • a roster/directory of our members

We’ll be working collaboratively, both on design and implementation, and plan to have something workable by the end of the day — whenever that is.

The codefest will take place in Portland at the Rose & Raindrop on 522 SE Grand [map]. We’ll gather at 11am, meet each other, set up, grab some food, and some planning. Then, on to coding! We figure it will last until early evening.

Have some thoughts? Want to code? Please contact John Labovitz for more information.
